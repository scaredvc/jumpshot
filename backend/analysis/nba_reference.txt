## Stephen Curry
Stephen Curry shoots from a compact but fluid base with a quick dip into the gather and a fast energy transfer from the floor through the release. His feet are usually set slightly staggered with good balance, and he is comfortable flowing into the shot off movement without a long pause at the set point. The ball path is efficient and tight to the body, his guide hand stays quiet, and the release is fast with a high finish and relaxed follow-through. The overall motion is smooth and coordinated rather than rigid, with a quick trigger that still preserves alignment through the shoulders and hips.

## Klay Thompson
Klay Thompson's form is typically more upright and repeatable, with a strong base, minimal wasted motion, and a very direct path from the gather to release. He gets to a clean set point early, keeps the ball centered, and relies on balance and timing more than improvisation. His release is compact and consistent, with a stable guide hand, squared shoulders, and a classic one-motion rhythm that looks controlled from start to finish. The shot profile emphasizes structure, repeatability, and clean mechanics over the freer off-platform style seen in movement shooters.
